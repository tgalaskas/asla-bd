Four Paths to an ARE Project

🤝
Path 1 — Cultivate the Prime Architects
The highest-leverage move. Build relationships with SGA, Payette, Gensler, HOK, Flad, and SHoP before they are engaged on ARE projects. These firms assemble the project team — including the landscape architect — before or shortly after receiving the commission. If you're already known and trusted by these primes, you're on the shortlist before the conversation with ARE even happens.

Action: Identify the science + technology practice leaders at Gensler San Diego, HOK Boston, and Flad SF. Invite them to coffee. Ask about their life science pipeline. Establish a working relationship.
📍
Path 2 — Target San Diego Specifically
Both confirmed ARE landscape architects (Surfacedesign, Bionic) are SF-based serving the Mission Bay campus. No LA firm is publicly confirmed on ARE's San Diego projects — which include the most active near-term development pipeline (Campus Point BMS expansion, Torrey Pines, UTC). San Diego is ARE's second-largest market and the most geographically accessible for a Southern California firm.

Action: Research which architects are working on Campus Point expansion. Attend NAIOP San Diego events where ARE development staff participate. Connect with the ARE San Diego regional team directly at industry events.
⚙️
Path 3 — Build the GC Relationship
Truebeck Construction has been GC on two confirmed ARE Mission Bay projects. General contractors influence subconsultant selection — especially for landscape, which is often bid and awarded during construction, not during design. A Truebeck relationship is a direct path to ARE landscape work in the Bay Area. BrightView (landscape contractor) executes the installed work — knowing who they work with tells you who designs it.

Action: Reach out to Truebeck's pre-construction team. Ask about their upcoming life science project pipeline. Being listed as an approved landscape architecture subconsultant puts you in their bid list for future ARE projects.
🌱
Path 4 — LEED and Sustainability as the Entry Point
ARE actively pursues LEED certification — 54% of rental revenue comes from LEED-certified or targeting properties. They won the 2024 Nareit Sustainable Design Impact Award. Landscape architects contribute critical LEED credits: stormwater management, heat island reduction, site ecology, outdoor quality. Frame your services as LEED site credit delivery, not landscape design.

Action: Develop a one-page summary of LEED credits your firm delivers on life science campuses, with specific examples. Use this as a conversation starter with ARE's sustainability team (SVP Eleni Reed) and with LEED-focused architects like Payette and HOK.

Research Methodology & Limitations
Confirmed firms are documented through public press releases, award submissions (Chicago Athenaeum, LILA), trade press (Tradeline, Metropolis, Bisnow), and project credit databases (mooool.com, The Plan). Likely firms are identified based on market presence, life science specialization, geographic proximity to ARE campuses, and known relationships within the ARE ecosystem (e.g., Kendall Square Association membership). This research represents publicly available information and may not capture private/undisclosed project teams, NDA-protected relationships, or projects that predate digital press coverage.
